Berkeley Hyperloop

The Hyperloop is a transportation concept that envisions sending a levitating aluminum pod 760 miles per hour to efficiently transport people between locations. Billionaire Elon Musk sponsored a competition to challenge student teams to build a functional pod and test it on his private track at SpaceX. Roles and Achievements

I led a team of 60+ UC Berkeley engineers to build a Hyperloop pod in six weeks and under a budget of $9k. I engaged leads at other student organization to learn about their manufacturing processes, and convinced vendors and suppliers to create partnerships and sponsorships. During the competition, I explained the team's design and manufacturing decisions to SpaceX engineers.

Due to budget restrictions, it was impossible to build a carbon fiber pod using the traditional process which was being used by most teams (female mold created with fiberglass and expensive CNC'ed foam). I used my experience as a Product Development Engineer to ideate a custom manufacturing process and design the required tooling. The solution was a male mold made with aluminum sheet metal and fixtures, much like the fuselage of modern planes.
